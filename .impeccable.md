## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are finance and operations staff working inside an internal contract lifecycle management product. They use the system to review contract ledgers, maintain financial records, upload and inspect attachments, export reports, and audit business activity. Their workflow is task-driven, dense, and repeatable, so the interface must reduce friction and support long sessions without fatigue.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel strictly professional, calm, and trustworthy. It should support concentration and precision rather than enthusiasm or novelty. Delight is appropriate only when it improves confidence, clarity, or perceived quality.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a restrained workspace aesthetic with disciplined neutrals, clear hierarchy, low-noise surfaces, and subtle accent color. The interface should not look like heavy enterprise gray; it needs warmth, contrast discipline, and enough visual refinement to feel current without becoming glossy or promotional. Both light and dark themes should preserve this tone.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize clarity over decoration.
2. Use warmth and tonal contrast to avoid lifeless enterprise gray.
3. Keep interaction feedback subtle, fast, and useful.
4. Let shared primitives define the system before page-level exceptions.
5. Maintain trust through consistency, density control, and accessible states.
